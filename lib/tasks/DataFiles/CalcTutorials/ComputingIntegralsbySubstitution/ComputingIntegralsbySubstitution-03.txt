#### Example \#3

We can evaluate \(\displaystyle\int\! \frac{dx}{(4x-3)^2}\) by letting 
\[ \begin{align} u = 4x-3 \; \rightarrow \; du &= 4\, dx\quad\longrightarrow \\ dx &= \frac{1}{4}\, du.\end{align} \]
Then \[\int \frac{dx}{(4x-3)^2}=\int \frac{\frac{1}{4}\, du}{u^2}=-\frac{1}{4u}+C=\frac{-1}{4(4x-3)}+C.\] 

It is not always apparent until you try it whether or not a substitution will work.
