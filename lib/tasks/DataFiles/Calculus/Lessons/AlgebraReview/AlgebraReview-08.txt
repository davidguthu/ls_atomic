\[ ax = bx, \hspace .5em  \text{ therefore } \hspace .5em  a = b \]

**MISTAKE!**

This is a more subtle mistake. The cancellation is correct IF \(x\) is not \(0\). For example \(2x = 3x\) forces \(x = 0\). You cannot cancel the \(x = 0\) and conclude that \(2 = 3\). Not in this universe, anyway.
