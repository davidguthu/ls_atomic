#### Example \#2

To compute \(\displaystyle\int\! \sin (2x)\cos (2x)\, dx\), let 
\[ \begin{eqnarray*} u = \sin (2x) \; \rightarrow \; du=2\cos (2x)\, dx. \end{eqnarray*} \]
Then 
\[\int \sin (2x)\cos (2x)\, dx=\int\frac{1}{2}\sin (2x)[2\cos (2x)\, dx]=\int \frac{1}{2}u\, du=\frac{1}{4}u^2+C=\frac{1}{4}\sin^2 (2x)+C.\] 

With practice, you will often be able to write down the result immediately.
